## Summary of Human Edit Patterns

### What humans fix most:

1. **Replace placeholders with real content** ‚Äî Every single AI placeholder ([DOWNLOAD LINK], [INSERT TIP], [Resource 1-3]) was either replaced with real content or the entire section was deleted. Never left as-is.
2. **Strip filler rather than fix it** ‚Äî When AI generates filler (fake resource lists, placeholder tips), humans prefer deleting the entire section over trying to improve it (edits 18, 19).
3. **Add educational substance** ‚Äî Generic check-ins become mini-articles with specific advice, data points, and actionable frameworks (edits 2, 5, 6, 12-15).
4. **Soften the hard sell** ‚Äî CTAs shift from "Hit reply!" to "Out of curiosity..." and "Feel free to..." (edits 13-15).
5. **Remove jargon and formality** ‚Äî "authority-calibrated" ‚Üí deleted, "reflexive" ‚Üí "natural", "frame our exchange" ‚Üí "set the scope of our discussion" (edits 24-31).
6. **Kill em dashes** ‚Äî Systematic replacement of em dashes with commas or parentheses across the Paris Kit (edits 27-29).
7. **Format for LinkedIn** ‚Äî Break dense paragraphs into single-statement lines and bullet lists (edits 32-33).
8. **Fix authenticity** ‚Äî Switch sender from intermediary to actual creator (edit 20-21). Use real names and real emails instead of templates (edit 15).

### Implications for AI generation:

- **Stop generating placeholders** ‚Äî if you can't fill it with real content, don't include the section
- **Default to shorter, punchier paragraphs** ‚Äî especially for LinkedIn posts
- **Use fewer em dashes** ‚Äî prefer commas and parentheses
- **Soften CTAs by default** ‚Äî "curious" > "hit reply", "feel free" > "let me know"
- **Avoid jargon** ‚Äî if a word wouldn't be used in casual conversation, find a simpler one
- **Add specifics or add nothing** ‚Äî generic resource lists are worse than no resources at all
- **Send emails from the content creator** ‚Äî not a platform operator or intermediary
